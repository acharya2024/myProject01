Question #07
A small block A of mass \(m\) is connected to spring of spring constant \(k\) and placed on smooth horizontal surface as shown. Another identical block B having same charge but of opposite polarity is placed at large distance. The block B is slowly moved towards the block A by some external agent, such that velocity of block B at all times is maintained constant. Find the elongation in the spring when the two blocks collide.
Answer \(3\left(\frac{q^2 }{16\pi {{\text{}\varepsilon\text{ }}_{\text{o}}k}}\right)^\frac{1}{3}\)
Explanation

The variation of spring force \(F_\text{spring}\) and the electrostatic force \(F_\text{Electrostatic}\) on the block A as a function of position \(x\) measured from its initial position is as shown in graph. The graph has been platted for a given separation between the blocks.

Since the graph shows two points of intersection of the two different forces, there are two possible positions of equilibrium of the block A for this given separation between the blocks.

If the separation between the blocks is now decreased by movement of block B towards A, the graph modifies in following manner:

At such given separation of blocks, the equilibrium for the block A is not possible. In this situation, since the electrostatic force on block A exceeds spring force on A at all possible positions, therefore the block will continue to move towards the block B. Such transition for block A, from being in equilibrium to being contineously attracted, occurs when the variations \(F_\text{E}\) and \(F_\text{S}\) shows following variations:

In this state, if the separation between the block is \(L\) and the elongation in the spring is \(x\), then at the point of equilibrium magnitude of forces as well as slopes of the forces in the graph are equal. Thus, mathematically we have:

\(\qquad kx=\frac{1}{4\pi {{\text{ }\!\!\varepsilon\!\!\text{ }}_{\text{o}}}}\frac{qq}{{{(l-x))}^{2}}}\) and \(\qquad k=2\frac{1}{4\pi {{\text{ }\!\!\varepsilon\!\!\text{ }}_{\text{o}}}}\frac{qq}{{{(l-x))}^{3}}}\)

\(\qquad k=2kx\frac{1}{{{(l-x))}}}\)\(\quad \Rightarrow\quad l-x=2x\)\(\qquad \Rightarrow\quad l=3x\)

\(\Rightarrow\quad x^3=\frac{q^2 }{16\pi {{\text{}\varepsilon\text{ }}_{\text{o}}k}}\) \(\Rightarrow\quad x=\left(\frac{q^2 }{16\pi {{\text{}\varepsilon\text{ }}_{\text{o}}k}}\right)^\frac{1}{3}\)

At this equilibrium position, the elongation in the spring is \(x\) and separation between the blocks is \(l\). If the block B is further brought close to A, the block A will contineously get accelerated towards B and will collide with B. So, the elongation in the spring during contact is \(l\). So, elongation at the time of contact of the two blocks is \(3\left(\frac{q^2 }{16\pi {{\text{}\varepsilon\text{ }}_{\text{o}}k}}\right)^\frac{1}{3}\)
