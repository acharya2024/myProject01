Question 1

The speed of light in a vacuum is approximately \(3 \times 10^8\) m/s. True or False?

Hint

Recall the universally accepted value for the speed of light.

Solution

The speed of light in a vacuum is a fundamental constant, approximately \(299,792,458\) m/s, which is often approximated as \(3 \times 10^8\) m/s.

Question 1

What is the name of the particle responsible for electromagnetic force?

Hint

It's a massless particle that carries light.

Solution

The photon is the elementary particle responsible for electromagnetic phenomena, including light.

Question 1

Which of the following is a scalar quantity?

Hint

Consider which quantities have magnitude only.

Solution

Speed is a scalar quantity as it has only magnitude. The others are vector quantities with both magnitude and direction.

Question 1

Which of the following are renewable energy sources? (Select all that apply)

Hint

Renewable energy sources are those that are replenished naturally.

Solution

Solar, wind, and hydroelectric power are renewable. Coal and natural gas are fossil fuels and non-renewable.

Question 1

Calculate the kinetic energy of a 2 kg object moving at a speed of 3 m/s.

Hint

Use the kinetic energy formula \( KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2 \).

Solution

\( KE = \frac{1}{2} \times 2 \, \text{kg} \times (3 \, \text{m/s})^2 = 9 \, \text{J} \).
